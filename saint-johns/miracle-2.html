The Second Miracle

March 11, 2019

It happened again.

I got another kid to shadow me at Saint John's.

But it almost happened earlier......


Side Story - February 12, 2019

It was Friday, February 8, and the Admissions lady walked in to my homeroom.

The host for Tuesday was absent, and she asked who else had the interests. She said she'd decide betwene me and someone else (who by the way is a teacher's pet)

I stopped by her office later that day, she told me that I wouldn't be getting this kid, mainly because I had Max the day before.

She also told me that Max's dad stopped by (who works at the school) and told her that I had done a great job.

The teacher's pet also didn't get the kid.

End Side Story


I'm in homeroom on March 8th.

She walks in, like she does everyday with a smile on her face, and calls my name. It was 8:i-didnt-look-at-the-clock am and I'm fully awake. I'm aware that I may have a math test (which was moved) and sign the paper.


Once Monday morning comes, I'm ready and I'm there before Amith (the kid's name). I go to homeroom and check in with Tony and tell him "I'm gonna get the...I'm not gonna repeat that line again". That line was "I'm gonna go get the kid" which I said before getting Max.

I had Amith for the entire day this time around, since I had no tests.

English - 1st Period

Okay, in English we took notes on verbals after a five minute vocab quiz.

Resource - 2nd Period

With Max I didn't have resource, it cycled out.

I took him to Campus Ministry, where they do morning prayer during 2nd period and also we ran into the 2 seniors I knew at the time (now I know 7), both named Mike. They are very nice people and I might actually write a seperate story on how we met.

We continued the tour in the Academic Center, which is where one can get tutoring, make up a test, or do homework.

I then took him to the Engineering Makerspace, which opened in the fall of 2017, that's a place where the Robotics team eets, students can 3D print stuff, and more.

I took him to the Library and introduced him to more friends and we stayed there for the rest of the period.

Religion - 3rd Period

I then took him to Religion class, with Mr. Paul. I forgot what we did.

Lunch

Good food. Much more confident.

Algebra 2 - 4th Period

Yes, Mr. G knows Amith's older brother, who transferred to a much better school for juniors and seniors.

He wasn't that bad at Algebra 2.

French - 5th Period

1. I didn't teach him a new word, but he was fine.

He, like Max, takes Spanish in middle school, and plans to continue here.

Biology - 6th Period

We were in the lab, yay.

The lab was something microscope-related, very hard.

I walked back to Admissions with him and we split.

The Admissions lady, during the drop off, said that I was "very enthusiastic and a great host".

Update, November 25, 2020: Amith is a f--king genius. He was in pre-calculus as a freshman, and is in my Java 2 class this year (as a sophomore). I haven't compared our heights yet.
